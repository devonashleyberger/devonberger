Versal

“A new way for anyone to teach virtually anything, interactively.”

Launched as a passion project for an already successful CEO, Versal set out to bring developers, instructors, and students together on one platform to shake up the way educational materials are consumed.

MARKETS: PRIVATE SCHOOL TEACHERS, PUBLIC SCHOOL TEACHERS, HOBBYISTS, CORPORATE TRAINERS

DESIGN LEAD

2013–2016

USER RESEARCH, USER EXPERIENCE, USER INTERFACE, BRANDING, PRODUCT MANAGEMENT

Versal offered an opportunity to innovate in a new and exciting market and be inspired by a brilliant team.

I worked with the founders and engineering team to launch a private beta for validation and feedback. Post launch, I continued innovating as the lead product designer to ensure that the shifting market and user needs were being met. Two years after leaving the company, designs I actively worked on were still being implemented and released.

The Product

Versal for Learners

Organizations, educators, and experts can invite learners to private courses, embed a course directly into a website or blog, or deliver courses via an LMS with LTI or SCORM.

Course Authoring

The market was new and online education was sticking to a typical structure (Lecture / Readings / Assessment) for course content. Versal’s mission was to build a community around developing “gadgets” as a means of distributing course material. Through the gadget marketplace and course creator, authors can arrange gadgets to effectively bring interaction and real time feedback to the traditionally static experience of course work.

Course Management

Courses can be made public or private to accomodate the variety of markets using Versal.

Course management is where organizations, educators, and experts can invite learners to private courses, embed a course directly into a website or blog, or deliver courses via an LMS with LTI or SCORM.
