Project

NoiseAware

UX Audit, User Research, & Redesign

Background

NoiseAware is a privacy-safe smart home solution that protects rental properties by monitoring noise levels. The service alerts the host and guest through a web or mobile experience when the noise levels go over a set threshold, allowing property managers to proactively address potential noise violations before they become a significant problem.

Hired to guide the product team in building a better user experience, I audited the full user journey, beginning with marketing/customer acquisition. The audit exposed inconsistencies in design language, weak UX writing, and a complex experience that was the result of applying endless hot-fixes to address customer requests.


Onboarding Audit

Planning and Management

Once the audit was reviewed, the background work of mapping mile-high user journeys for the existing experience and workshopping pain points with the customer service team took place. This way, the design team could focus on projects that would have a significant impact while we began organizing user interviews for queuing up future projects.

Documentation is then created, circulated, and updated so that there is a source of true for all work.


Improved Onboarding Experience

Research revealed user confusion during set up that lead to unnecessary customer service calls during hardware setup at rental properties. We addressed this by creating a guided experience, asking property managers simple questions to determine the threshold settings. I led the product and engineering teams in this redesign, initially focusing on improving the setup and onboarding experience and later moving on to core product features.
